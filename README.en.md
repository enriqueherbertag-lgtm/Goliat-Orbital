# Goliat-Orbital: Space debris turned into raw materials

More than 130 million pieces of debris orbit the Earth. They travel at 28,000 km/h. A 1 cm fragment has the energy of a car at 50 km/h. If nothing is done, the Kessler Syndrome will make low orbits unusable.

Current missions hunt one object at a time, at a million-dollar cost. This is not a future problem. It is a current problem that grows every year.

**Goliat-Orbital was created to solve it.**

## What it is

Goliat-Orbital is a cylindrical spacecraft with six longitudinal gills. It orbits in the same direction as the debris, with zero relative velocity. It detects fragments hundreds of meters away and sucks them in without impact. Once inside, it compacts or melts them in artificial gravity, producing aluminum, copper, iron-tungsten, and silver ingots.

The cycle is closed: the spacecraft feeds on atmospheric gas to maintain its orbit (air-breathing engine) and recovers energy from the process (residual heat, gas flow, rotation). It has no fragile solar panels or external dependencies.

## What it does

- **Impact-free capture**: six gills with compressors create a gas flow that pulls fragments inside. Zero relative velocity.
- **Active protection**: 2.54 cm Space Armor shielding resists impacts from 3 mm fragments at 7 km/s.
- **Compaction or melting**: in phase 1, it compacts fragments into scrap ingots. In phase 2, it melts them in artificial gravity (rotating module) and injection-molds them.
- **Storage and sale**: ingots are stored in a rotating warehouse with digital inventory. They can be re-entered to Earth or transferred in orbit.
- **Energy self-sufficiency**: uses atmospheric gas for propulsion (air-breathing engine) and recovers energy from residual heat, gas flow, and rotation.

## Who it is for

- **Space agencies**: solution for cleaning low and medium orbits cost-effectively.
- **Satellite operators**: reduced collision risk.
- **Space industry**: raw materials (aluminum, copper, iron-tungsten, silver) produced in orbit.
- **Governments and defense**: Kessler Syndrome mitigation.

## Main advantages

- **Mass capture**: does not hunt one object at a time; it sucks up every fragment in its path.
- **No fragile solar panels**: feeds on atmospheric gas and recovers energy from the process.
- **Closed cycle**: no dependence on external resources (fuel, energy).
- **Generates value**: turns space debris into sellable raw materials.
